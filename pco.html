Questions

Q: Tell us about a situation where you made a mistake when leading a team. What happened and how did you deal with it?

A: My first soirée into managing a product team was also my first real encounter with working as part of a remote team. I was new to the role and new to the company. With a very active boss, it became easy to get so wrapped up in my own workload that I wasn't making myself available to my team. Certain aspects of the project began struggling and for the most part it stemmed from either lack of direction, someone feeling stuck and not knowing how to ask for help, or simply a lack of awareness of blind spots with no one to help bring a fresh perspective. Luckily one of the teams developers called me out. We talked about what he thought the solution could be and presented it to the team. The team agreed that I would schedule checkins with the expectation that each would take less than 5-10 minutes, unless more time was warranted.


Q: Give us an example of a product that you love or you hate and why.

A: Oh, wow. I think right now it has to be YNAB. My wife will tell you that budgeting has not always been my strong suit, but after 3+ years using this tool, seeing their growth, and watching the way they listen to and care for their customers, I'm sold.


They have created a clean, intuitive tool, that has made my life easier, and their reporting tools are actually fun. Nerdy, nerdy fun.

Q: Tell us about a situation where you had to influence others who disagreed with you. What happened and how did you deal with it?

A: I had been in my current role as a customer service rep for about three months. The company is small and has been around for 30+ years. In the past 5 years they've seen turnover of ownership, fluctuating product focus, and an unhealthy view from the target audience, none of which had been given the attention they needed. As a consumer of the product, and now an employee of the company, I was itching to do something about it. I was told there was no time or money to spend on a rebrand, and no way we could change perception. "It is what it is, we just need to find new customers."

Being that I can be slightly... stubborn, I started reaching out to friends that new the brand. I found a designer and a copywriter that were willing to help me out in exchange for beer and food while we worked and the possibility of a product trade if we won over my team. After we dialed in our proposal I brought in our product director and VP of sales and pitched our work. We won them over not because it was perfect, but because we showed care, patience, took direction, and stayed positive. After having been at the company 8 months at this point we landed on new iconography, a new product line, and a brand/marketing guide. Patience, care, and communication won out.
